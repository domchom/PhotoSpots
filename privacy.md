PhotoSpots Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

PhotoSpots is built to respect your privacy. This policy explains what data the app uses and why.

Data You Create
All photo spots, lists, notes, and photos you add are stored on your device. If iCloud sync is enabled on your device, this data is also synced privately to your iCloud account using Apple's CloudKit infrastructure. This data is yours. It is not shared with third parties, and the developer does not have access to your private iCloud data.

Location
PhotoSpots requests access to your device's location only to show your current position on the map, calculate distances to saved spots, and support location-based features such as saving a spot from the widget. Your live device location is not sold, shared with third parties, or used for analytics.

Photos and Camera
PhotoSpots can access your photo library and camera only when you choose to attach photos to a spot. Attached photos are stored locally and, if iCloud sync is enabled, in your private iCloud data. They are not uploaded to any server outside of Apple's infrastructure.

Notifications
PhotoSpots may request notification permission for app features such as confirming that a spot was saved from the widget or imported in the background. Notification content is generated on-device and is not sent to the developer or any third party.

In-App Purchases
Tips and any in-app purchases are processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit. The developer does not collect or store payment information.

Analytics and Tracking
PhotoSpots does not use third-party analytics SDKs, does not track you across apps or websites, and does not sell your data.

Third-Party Services
PhotoSpots relies on Apple services such as iCloud/CloudKit, Photos, Maps URL handling, and StoreKit to provide core functionality. No third-party advertising or analytics frameworks are included in the app.
